3D-Printed In-Vitro Chronic Wound Model

Rutgers BME Research Lab

We developed a 3D-printed stamp based in vitro assay to model epithelial wounds by creating uniform, reproducible abrasions in keratinocyte monolayers under low-nutrient conditions. The stamp generates four consistent scratches per well in a 96-well plate, achieving less than 10% variation and enabling high-throughput, resource-efficient testing.

Compared to traditional 10 micro-liter pipette-tip methods, this system significantly improves reproducibility and quantitative analysis of wound closure.
