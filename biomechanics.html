Pregnancy Biomechanics Analysis

Introduction to Biomechanics (14:125:308)

For the final project in Introduction to Biomechanics (14:125:308), our team analyzed the effects of different stages of gestation on lower back pain. While lower back pain is often reported early in pregnancy, its biomechanical causes evolve as gestation progresses.

Using free-body diagrams and static equilibrium modeling, we examined how changes in spinal curvature, body weight, and center of mass across trimesters influence spinal forces. Our analysis showed that changes in lumbar spinal curvature, particularly in the later stages of pregnancy, are the dominant contributor to increased back pain.

These findings highlight the importance of biomechanical modeling in understanding pregnancy-related discomfort and informing potential mitigation strategies.
