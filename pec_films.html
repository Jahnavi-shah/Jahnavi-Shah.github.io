Polyelectrolyte Complex Film Dressing

Rutgers BME Research Lab

Polyelectrolyte therapeutic films for wound healing: Chronic skin wounds are epithelial injuries, requiring wound dressings that support epithelial regeneration and sustained biochemical signaling. We developed a polymer-based polyelectrolyte complex (PEC) film dressing designed for controlled delivery of bioactive nanoparticles such as MSH-Œ± to promote wound healing.

The PEC films are composed of biocompatible natural polymers and enable slow, graded release of elastin-like polypeptide (ELP)-based growth factor nanoparticles. Swelling, degradation, and release studies demonstrated tunable mechanical stability and sustained delivery under wound-mimicking conditions.
