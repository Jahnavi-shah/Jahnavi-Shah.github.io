Schlenk Line Development for Karstedt Catalyst Synthesis

Ethicon (Johnson & Johnson) ¬∑ Industrial R&D Project

During my internship at Ethicon (Johnson & Johnson), I developed a custom Schlenk line setup to support the synthesis of Karstedt catalyst, a platinum- based organometallic compound that is extremely sensitive to oxygen and moisture. The system was designed to provide continuous access to an inert nitrogen atmosphere while enabling precise vacuum control for solvent removal.

A critical requirement of the process was achieving a reduced pressure of approximately 15 mmHg to fully remove residual solvent from the catalyst solution. Even trace solvent contamination was found to introduce impurities that adversely affected catalyst performance. The setup therefore integrated both inert gas handling and controlled vacuum application.

To prevent environmental exposure during transfers and reagent addition, cannulation techniques were employed throughout the synthesis. This allowed solutions to be transferred and new reagents introduced without breaking the inert atmosphere, ensuring catalyst purity and reproducibility.

Custom Schlenk Line Configuration: Shown is the multi-port Schlenk line developed to support Karstedt catalyst synthesis under an inert nitrogen atmosphere. The setup integrates parallel reaction flasks, vacuum control capable of reaching approximately 15 mmHg for solvent removal, and septum-sealed access points for cannulation-based reagent transfer. This configuration enabled oxygen-free handling of highly reactive organometallic species and minimized contamination during synthesis.
