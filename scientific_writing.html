Addressing Physician Burnout and Turnover at RWJBarnabas Health

Scientific and Technical Writing ¬∑ Project Proposal

This project was developed as part of a Scientific and Technical Writing course and focused on proposing a system-level intervention to address physician burnout and turnover at RWJBarnabas Health (RWJBH). Rather than framing burnout as an individual failure, the proposal approached it as a structural and organizational challenge requiring coordinated policy, leadership, and workforce interventions.

The report synthesized peer-reviewed literature to evaluate evidence-based strategies including mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR), peer support programs, leadership training, and workload optimization through flexible scheduling. Economic considerations were central to the proposal, with detailed analysis of physician replacement costs, productivity loss, and long-term retention benefits.

A comprehensive implementation plan was developed, outlining funding pathways, projected costs, and anticipated outcomes related to workforce stability, physician well-being, and patient care quality. This project demonstrates the ability to translate research findings into actionable, budget-conscious policy recommendations within complex healthcare systems.
